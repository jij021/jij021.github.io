How was this home generated?

The survey questions correspond to one of different spheres (also seen in your result). Your answers are tallied, and an abstract representation of a “home” is generated based off of those results.

Each part of the generated home is also based off of these spheres, which build on top of each other.

What does my result mean?

The parts of the house change based on how strongly you responded to certain questions.

These changes are created on a scale based off of the 4 spheres exploring the different meanings of home.

Generally, more organic shapes indicate a more theoretical view of home, while more geometric shapes indicate a more grounded view of home.

Behavioral

(roof)

Lower score

“I don’t really care how much I’m able to do at home”

Higher score

“Home means I can do whatever I want”

Physical

(base)

Lower score

“Home is more of an experience or state of mind than a place”

Higher score

“Home is somewhere I can physically be”

Social

(style/number of windows)

Lower score

“Being with others isn’t a priority when I’m at home”

Higher score

“Social interactions are very important to me at home”

Cognitive/Emotional

(shape in windows)

Lower score

“Home is more just a place for me to stay”

Higher score

“Home needs to stir many emotions and thoughts for me”

These parts are combined to form your generated result based off of these scales.

Regardless of your result, the topic of “home” is endlessly complex. These results are not meant to define you or your view.

Rather, they show a part of your thoughts and unique identity, contributing to the larger idea of what 'home' may mean to many different people.
